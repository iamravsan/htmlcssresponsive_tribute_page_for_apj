Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Here is the timeline of Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam's life

  • 1931 - Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, also known as Dr A.P.J. Abdul Kalam at Rameshwaram in Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 - He Graduated from Saint Joseph's College Trichy
  • 1955 - Joined Aeronautical Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1960 - Finishes College Studies and joined Aerospace Establishment of Indian Defence Research & Developement Services
  • 1969 - Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
  • 1963 - Visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia
  • 1970 - Kalam was directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant
  • 1992 - Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation
  • 1997 - Received India's highest Civilian Award Baratha Rathna
  • 1998 - Invented low cost "Coronary stent" along with Soma raju Named it Kalam Raju Stent
  • 2002 - Kalam served as the 11th President of India
  • 2009 - Received International von Kármán Wings Award California Institute of Technology, USA
  • 2011 - IEEE Honorary Membership
  • 2013 - Received Von Braun Award from National Space Society
  • 2014 - Received Honorary Doctor of Science Form Edinburgh University
  • 2014 - Worked as Honorary professor Beijing University, China
  • 2015 - Dies at the age of 83

"our country has lost a great human being who made phenomenal contributions to the promotion of self reliance in defence technologies. I worked very closely with Dr. Kalam as prime minister and I greatly benefited from his advice as president of our country. His life and work will be remembered for generations to come"
